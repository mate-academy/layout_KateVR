Will it decrease my motion sickness?

Motion sickness is caused by a mismatch between the visual cues that the eyes are receiving and the motion signals that the inner ear is sensing. This can occur in virtual reality and gaming scenarios where the visual environment is moving, but the user's body is not. The Kat Loco system helps to reduce this mismatch by providing more accurate and responsive motion tracking, which can help reduce the symptoms of motion sickness.

However, it is important to note that motion sickness is a complex and individualized phenomenon, and different people may respond differently to different interventions. Some users may find that the Kat Loco system is effective at reducing their motion sickness symptoms, while others may not see any improvement. Additionally, it is always important to take breaks and limit your exposure to virtual reality or gaming environments if you are prone to motion sickness.

Which VR headsets is it compatible with?

Kat Loco uses a USB dongle to connect to your VR headset, so as long as your headset has a USB port and is compatible with the Kat Loco software, it should work. However, it's always a good idea to check the compatibility of your specific VR headset with the Kat Loco system before purchasing to ensure that it will work as expected. Additionally, Kat Loco offers a software development kit (SDK) that allows developers to integrate the Kat Loco system into their own VR applications.

Is it compatible with Oculus Quest?

Yes, Kat Loco is compatible with Oculus Quest and Oculus Quest 2. To use Kat Loco with the Oculus Quest, you need to connect the Kat Loco dongle to the Oculus Quest using a USB-C to USB-A adapter. You will also need to download and install the Kat Loco software onto your computer, and then use it to calibrate the Kat Loco system.

Once the Kat Loco system is set up and calibrated, you can use it to enhance your VR experience on the Oculus Quest. The Kat Loco system provides accurate and responsive motion tracking, which can help reduce motion sickness and improve the overall immersive experience of using the Oculus Quest.

It's worth noting that while Kat Loco is compatible with Oculus Quest and Quest 2, it is not an official Oculus accessory and is not endorsed or supported by Oculus. However, many users have reported success using the Kat Loco system with their Oculus Quest.
